INTRODUCTION

The Imperial College Data Science Society is an opportunity for students to engage with and learn about Data Science. Data science which encompasses computer science and mathematics--specifically Machine Learning (ML), Artificial Intelligence (AI), statistics, and their applications toward solving problems in various domains--has been one of the hottest topics in the second half of this decade.

Being the departmental society of the Imperial College Data Science Institute and with a student reach across all major departments, the Imperial College Data Science Society (ICDSS) promotes data science awareness amongst the student body. While most people have heard of ML and AI, many are still unfamiliar with their applications in business and other domains through data science.

PURPOSE AND GOALS

Bridging the Gaps

ICDSS’s goal is to equip students from all disciplines with key data science skills and bridge the gap between industry and academia. The talents we develop help meet the high demands of various industries for data scientists. We do this by providing students with industry insights, skill development workshops, Hackathons, and projects to practice the art of data science.

Data for Good

We raise the awareness on being ethical in using tehcnology and applying our skills to make real impact to communities. We support non-for-profits by providing analytics service for free. We are actively considering data privacy for all of our activities, which can reassure our members and our clients.

WHAT WE OFFER

Workshops

We aim to expand our workshop series by inviting our
sponsors and relevant companies to teach essential Data Science skills.

Hackathons

We host AI Hack annually, the largest student-led AI Hackathon in the UK.

Advanced Analytics Consulting Projects

Providing hands-on data science experience for students to tackle real-world problems in industrial settings.

Industrial Networking

Regular talks by Data Scientists from industry provide our members with insights into the real-world applications of Data Science.

AI Hack is Imperial College Data Science Society's annual datathon where students get to showcase their analytics skills and meet like-minded aspiring data scientists.
AI Hack will take place at the South Kensington Campus of Imperial College London on the weekend of the 29th of February — 1st of March.
To be able to produce a good submission, we would recommend some familiarity with some of the following things:

  • Data and software engineering: pandas, dplyr, ggplot, matplotlib, seaborn, PyTorch, TensorFlow, Tableau, parallelisation, docker, GCP or AWS, ipython…
  • Machine learning: Nonparametric smoothing, random forests, finite and infinite mixture models, XGboost, kernel methods, Gaussian processes, neural networks, autoencoders…
  • Statistical modelling: frequentist and Bayesian linear models, frequentist and Bayesian hypothesis testing, Monte Carlo methods, bootstrap simulations, PCA…
We recommend Python, but you could also use R, Julia or any language that can be used for scientific computing.
We will provide:

  • Board extensions
  • Mentors from industry and our advisory team
Teams consist of 3-4 people
You will get the opportunity to meet new people and teammates at a pre-datathon networking event.
Sadly we won't be able to accommodate people who miss the sign-up date.
